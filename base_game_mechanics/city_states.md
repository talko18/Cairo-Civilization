# City-States

City-states are independent single-city entities that cannot win the game but provide
significant bonuses to civilizations that cultivate relationships with them.

## City-State Types

| Type | Color | Bonus Focus | Suzerain Bonus Examples |
|------|-------|-------------|------------------------|
| Scientific | Blue | Science | +20% Science in Capital, bonus Science from Campuses |
| Cultural | Purple | Culture | +2 Culture from Wonders, bonus Tourism |
| Industrial | Orange | Production | +2 Production in Capital from Envoys, bonus district Production |
| Militaristic | Red | Military | Unique units, bonus XP, Production toward military |
| Religious | White | Faith | +2 Faith in Holy Site cities, bonus religious pressure |
| Trade | Yellow | Gold | +4 Gold in Capital, bonus Trade Route yields |

## Envoy System

### Earning Envoys
| Source | Envoys |
|--------|--------|
| Influence Points accumulation | 1 Envoy per threshold (100 points base) |
| Civic completions | Some civics grant free Envoys |
| Certain Wonders | e.g., Apadana (+2 Envoys when a Wonder is built in the same city) |
| Policy Cards | e.g., Diplomatic League (+1 Envoy on meeting a city-state) |

### Envoy Thresholds and Bonuses

| Envoys | Bonus |
|--------|-------|
| 1 | Tier 1 bonus (applies to Capital only) |
| 3 | Tier 2 bonus (applies to cities with the matching district) |
| 6 | Tier 3 bonus (applies to cities with the matching district) |

#### Example: Scientific City-State
- 1 Envoy: +2 Science in your Capital
- 3 Envoys: +2 Science in every city with a Campus
- 6 Envoys: +2 Science in every city with a Campus (additional, stacks with 3-envoy bonus)

### Sending Envoys
- Envoys are sent to specific city-states from the city-state screen
- Once placed, Envoys are **permanent** (cannot be recalled)
- Exception: Certain spy missions and civilization abilities can remove Envoys

## Suzerainty

The player with the **most Envoys** (minimum 3) in a city-state becomes its **Suzerain**.

### Suzerain Benefits
| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Unique Bonus | Each city-state has a unique Suzerain bonus (see examples below) |
| Levy Military | Pay Gold to temporarily control the city-state's military units |
| Open Borders | Free passage through city-state territory |
| Resources | Access to the city-state's strategic and luxury resources |

### Levying Military

- Pay a Gold cost to control the city-state's military for 30 turns
- Levied units move under your control but still belong to the city-state
- After 30 turns, units return to city-state control
- Sumeria's Gilgamesh pays 50% less for levying

## City-State Interactions

### Positive Actions
- Sending Envoys increases your relationship
- Becoming Suzerain grants significant benefits
- Completing city-state quests earns free Envoys

### Quests
City-states issue quests to civilizations:
| Quest Type | Examples |
|-----------|---------|
| Build a specific district | "Build a Campus" |
| Train a specific unit | "Train a Swordsman" |
| Send a Trade Route | "Establish a Trade Route to us" |
| Convert to a religion | "Bring your religion to our city" |
| Recruit a Great Person | "Recruit a Great Scientist" |
| Trigger a specific Eureka/Inspiration | "Earn the Eureka for X technology" |

Completing a quest grants **+1 Envoy** with that city-state.

### Declaring War on City-States
- You can declare war on a city-state to conquer it
- This removes the city-state from the game permanently
- Other civilizations may react negatively
- The city-state's Suzerain gains a **Protectorate War** casus belli against you

### City-State Territory
- After discovering **Early Empire** civic, you cannot enter city-state territory
  without being their Suzerain, Open Borders, or at war
